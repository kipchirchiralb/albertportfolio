What is Scope?

Project scope is the detailed outline of all work required to complete a project successfully. It is the boundary that defines what is included and, crucially, what is excluded.

IT Context Example:

"Includes specific software features, hardware installations, or database migrations."

1. Objectives

The specific goals the project aims to achieve (e.g., Increase efficiency by 20%).

2. Deliverables

Tangible products or results (e.g., A mobile app, a network audit report).

3. Constraints

Limitations like budget caps, technical requirements, or tight deadlines.

4. Exclusions

Explicitly stating what the project will NOT do to prevent misunderstanding.

Scope Creep: The "Project Killer"

Scope creep refers to the uncontrolled growth of a project's scope without adjustments to time, cost, or resources. It typically occurs incrementally during the Execution phase. Why does it happen?

Relative frequency of common scope creep causes based on IT project analysis.

Vague Initial Requirements

If the scope isn't documented clearly at the start, interpretation varies, leading to expanding expectations.

"Gold Plating"

When developers add "neat" extra features that weren't requested. While well-intentioned, this consumes budget and time without authorization.

Lack of Stakeholder Involvement

Missing key input from the people who will actually use the IT system results in late-stage change requests.

The Cost of Uncontrolled Growth

When scope expands (Creep) but resources and time remain static, the project enters a danger zone. This divergence usually leads to quality degradation, missed deadlines, or project failure.

  • Planned Scope: Linear, predictable growth.
  • Actual Scope (Creep): Exponential additions.
  • Budget/Resources: Fixed (The limitation).
